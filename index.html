Welcome! I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences at the University of California, Los Angeles. My curiousities lie in the dynamic ocean and its role in the Earth system! I am also a Freediver, and I love to feel the change of water temperature and pressure when I dive into the ocean.


PhD research: I aim to constrain dissipation of oceanic mesoscale energy, which is at length scale $O(100s\ \mathrm{km})$ and at time scale $O(1\ \mathrm{day})$. I am co-advised by Prof. Andrew Stewart and Prof. James McWilliams during the time at UCLA.

Postdoctoral research interests: I am interested in performing nonlinear internal waves simulations, predicting internal tides, and simulate submesoscale in bottom boundary.

others: I've been using Unmanned Airbone Systems to study coastal ocean and wave energy dissipation. Recently, I have a collaboration project and am interested in using machine learning techniques to predict bottom flow.

Send me an email at sunnyyeh@atmos.ucla.edu for collaboration, questions, mentoring, or Freediving! More details can be found in the respective sections of this website.
